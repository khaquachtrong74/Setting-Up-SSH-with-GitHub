Build Your Own System

In the book Atomic Habits, the author explains that achieving success isn’t just about setting goals—it's about building a system that supports consistent progress.

Goals set the direction, but systems drive progress.

Focus on creating a system of habits and routines that align with your long-term vision. It’s not the result that matters most; it’s the process that leads to it.


The way keep move forward?

It is a process of improve myself. It really really easy to make you go to wrong road.

I’ve developed small habits to improve myself, like exercising every afternoon, cooking to learn about nutrition, reading books to clear my mindset, and cleaning my room daily. Maintaining good habits every day brings you closer to your goals. My advice is to keep your mind clear—it helps you avoid bad habits. When you make positive choices, your energy grows incrementally, but a single bad decision can reset your progress to zero.

Improvement isn’t easy. You’ll feel tired, bored, or even depressed at times. That’s normal—it’s part of the process. Dopamine plays a significant role in this journey, as it can lift your mood temporarily but also drag you down afterward. We get dopamine from many activities: playing games, pursuing hobbies, reading, or learning new skills. However, these highs are fleeting, and your energy eventually depletes, leaving you drained.

So, how can we sustain a positive mood? The answer lies in consistently resetting your mind. Whenever you feel overwhelmed, take time to clear your thoughts—it works better than relying on dopamine. Additionally, regular exercise not only improves your physical health but also boosts your energy levels, sharpens your focus, and helps you sleep better.
